Cultural Blog - Emotional Intelligence


Research EQ and IQ


What is Emotional Intelligence?


Simply put Emotional intelligence is often defined as the ability to perceive, use, understand and handle emotions and the capacity to be aware/control and express one’s emotions. To handle interpersonal relationships with good judgement or sense and empathetically


How is it different to IQ?


IQ tests measure your ability to solve problems using logic and communication of complex ideas. IQ is a number derived from a standardised intelligence test.


IQ represents abilities such as:

  • -Visual and spatial processing
  • -Knowledge of the world
  • -Fluid reasoning
  • -Working memory and short-term memory
  • -Quantitative reasoning

EQ tests measure your ability to recognize emotions in others and yourself, using that awareness to make your decisions. Emotional intelligence refers to a person’s ability to perceive, control, evaluate and express one emotion.


EQ is centred on abilities such as:

  • -Identifying emotions
  • -Evaluating how others feel
  • -Controlling one’s own emotions
  • -Perceiving how others feel
  • Using emotions to facilitate social communication
  • -Relating to others


Why is it important?


Emotional intelligence (EQ) is important because if you had a very high IQ and no EQ you basically wouldn’t be able to function in the real world as working with people and having friends is a big part of life as well as your mental state.

I think Emotional intelligence is the key to personal and professional success and a balance of both EQ and IQ will take you far in life being good with people and intellectually smart.

A good example to represent why EQ is as important as IQ would be in a job interview.

If you were going for a Web Development interview up against someone with a really high IQ who knew all the ins and outs of Web development, however their people skills (EQ) were lacking so they would be a hard person to work within a team environment.

On the other hand, you have a higher EQ and are really good at working with others in a team but are not totally confident in your Web Development skills but are willing to work hard. You are more likely to get highered.
